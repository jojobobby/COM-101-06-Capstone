Two-Way Processing

What It Is: Communication is not one-way. It requires both a sender and a receiver with feedback to confirm the message is understood.

Example: In class, a teacher asks “Do you understand?” and we nod collectively. That nod is feedback, we never spoke verbally but the message has been received and acknowledged.

Why It Matters: Without feedback, we don’t know if we’ve been understood. That’s how misunderstandings happen, especially in emails or texts where verbal and physical feedback is often undetected.
