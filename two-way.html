Two-Way Processing

What It Is: Communication is not one-way. It requires both a sender and a receiver — with feedback to confirm the message is understood.

Example: In class, a teacher asks “Do you understand?” and a student nods. That nod is feedback — the message has been received and acknowledged.

Why It Matters: Without feedback, we don’t know if we’ve been understood. That’s how misunderstandings happen — especially in emails or texts.
